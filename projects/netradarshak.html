Netradarshak — AI -Powered Assistive Spectacles

Technologies Used

Raspberry Pi 4

Python 3

Computer Vision

ViT2 (Transformers)

Easy OCR

TCP Streaming

Netradarshak is a pair of smart spectacles that provide continuous, context aware verbal guidance to people with severe visual impairment. A Raspberry Pi 4 captures video, relays it with low latency TCP to an Android phone for heavy inference, and returns high precision audio feedback.

System Overview

The design is partitioned into five layers. Capturing and basic pre -processing occur on the Pi, while the phone handles three concurrent models (Face ID, scene captioning, OCR). The result is played back through a low -latency TTS engine. All services are containerised to simplify field upgrades.

Key Capabilities

  • Face Recognition: 128-D embeddings (FaceNet -Mobile) yield 94 % top -1 accuracy on a 500 -image in -house set.
  • Scene Captioning: ViT2 -Base, fine -tuned on MS -COCO, generates natural -language descriptions at 6 -8 fps.
  • Document Reading: Easy OCR with confidence gating (≥ 85 %) for real -time narration of printed text.
  • Bidirectional Control: Users can issue voice commands or touch gestures to switch modes or repeat the last description.

Hardware Highlights

  • 8 -MP Sony IMX219 camera, 12° downward tilt for natural POV.
  • Two Samsung INR -18650 30Q cells supply ≈ 3 h 48 m of continuous operation.
  • SLS -printed PA -12 housing keeps total on -face weight under 90 g.

Performance Metrics

  • End to -end latency: 326 ms ± 27 ms (camera ➝ speech).
  • Mean power draw: 5.6 W; SoC temperature remains below 65 °C with passive copper shim.

Engineering Challenges & Solutions

  • Frame drops under peak traffic: mitigated by enlarging TCP buffers to 512 KB and adding back -pressure signalling.
  • Thermal throttling after 45 min: resolved with a 0.8 mm copper shim and arm frequency cap at 1.2 GHz.
  • Camera driver mismatch: migrated from deprecated raspistill to the libcamera stack and patched overlays.

Planned Enhancements

  • Obstacle detection via a three -sensor time -of -flight array (effective range 0 -2 m).
  • Offline wake -word engine for hands -free mode switching.
  • Quantised on -device models to remove smartphone dependency for core functionality.
  • Solar trickle -charging attachment for extended outdoor use.
